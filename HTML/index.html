The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower was built to be one the main attractions at the Paris World's Fair in 1889.

That year, the World's Fair covered the entire Champ de Mars in Paris and its focus was the vast constructions in iron and steel that were the great industrial advancement of that time.

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron tower that stands 1,063 ft (324 m) tall in Paris.

Currently, the Eiffel Tower is the most-visited monument in the world with over 7 million visitors a year.

Visitors can choose to go up using the Tower lift or the stairs. There are 1,665 steps to the top.
